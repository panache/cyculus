#VirtualRealityMountainBiking

Get ready for the most exciting cardio experience ever.

The Problem

In today's hectic world, keeping active and healthy is increasingly difficult. With minimal time to engage in fun and exciting sports that drive the motivation to stay fit, many people resort to the confined and boring gym.

While fun ways to exercise exist, they often come with their own restrictions. They are often seasonal and dangerous, and hence are not ideal. We seek to create a cardiovascular exercise experience that is exciting, safe and accessible.

Our Solution

We have designed a virtual reality mountain bike simulator. Users can sit on our custom made rig, put on an Oculus Rift and explore virtual mountain bike trails. There are four major components to our system, as described below.

Our mechanical rig has been designed to be sturdy and handle all the needed loads. Made with machined steel, the base has the necessary truss support to bear the load of the bike and heavy person. A modifed bike is attached to with a pivot at the back, and we use a combination center of gravity and a a motor to provide the necessary stability. We have also included hard stops to ensure that users remain safe.

The pivot system provides the major haptic feedback required for the project to provide a unique exciting experience. We use a 24V power supply and a Jaguar controller to drive a motor with a 500:1 speed reduction ratio to control the angle of the bike. We employ a PID control scheme to ensure we are following the angles set by our physics engine.

The pedal resistance system helps make our system feel realistic to the user. By giving us the ability to increase or decrease the resistance provided to the user for pedalling. This is done by controlling the PWM duty cycle we pass through our custom controler to our 48V 1000W motor. We have also included a flywheel geared and chained to simulate the inertia associated with starting and rolling with a bike.

The final part of our system is the virtual environment. Developed with Unity, our levels allow the users to explore fantastic worlds resplendant with natural features. We read information from sensors on the bike to control the in-game model, which in turn passes parameters through our physics engine to our actuators on the rig.
